My Journey

I completed my first twelve years of education and one year of college in China. I initially chose to study business administration because I wasn’t sure of my true interests. However, after reflecting on my life and academic path during a gap year and freshman year in college, I discovered my genuine passion for neuroscience.

During my first summer break, I participated in the Greenheart Exchange Program through my college, which allowed me to work in Cody, Wyoming, and New York City. I fell in love with the vibrant and diverse atmosphere of NYC, just like I expected even before arriving. Although I wanted to continue my undergraduate studies in NYC, my family could not financially support me back then.

For the first time, I could legally use Google Maps, and I found myself constantly zooming in and out of the world map, wondering about the 99.9% area on the earth that I have never been to. I decided to explore South America and possibly continue my undergraduate study in a Spanish-speaking university.

I learned Spanish online while living nomadically across South America for over a year. I also taught myself programming online and worked remotely as a junior web developer. This travel experience is the most transformative in my life and has carved a global citizen identity into my genes. Although I never spared much time to document the experience, I have a profile and some records in Couchsurfing (jackongtwistio), which I used to connect with local people and travelers.

In 2023, I earned my Spanish DELE B2 certificate, which opened the door to potential studies at Spanish-speaking universities. Unfortunately, unexpected obstacles related to immigration disrupted those plans. Given the uncertainty, I started to pursue online bachelor’s degrees while figuring out where to go next. At the end of 2023, I discovered that my strongest affection for a person in NYC was reciprocated. So now, I’m living with my favorite human being in my favorite city, working toward the career of my dreams. Life is full of possibilities, and I couldn’t be more grateful.
